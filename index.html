Introduction

NeoEng+ (Zetanese) is a simplified version of English. It uses shortened words and unique grammar rules to make communication fast and efficient. Here's an explanation of its grammar and structure.

Grammar Overview

1. Tenses

In NeoEng+, tenses are marked using special symbols:

  • Past: Use ` before the verb (e.g., I `go|)
  • Present: Use ' after the verb (e.g., I 'go|)
  • Future: Use " before the verb (e.g., I "go|)

2. Sentence Structure

Sentences are kept simple with subject-verb-object structure. Tenses are determined by symbols and intonation.

  • Subject: Person or thing doing the action.
  • Verb: Action being performed.
  • Object: Thing receiving the action.

3. Shortened Words

Words are shortened for faster communication. Examples:

  • Hello → Hlo
  • Friend → Frd
  • Love → Lv
  • Water → Wtr

Direct Translation

English to Zetanese (NeoEng+)

English: "I will go now."

Zetanese: "I "go|"

Explanation: The future tense is marked by the symbol ", indicating an action that will take place.

More Examples

  • English: "I see my friend." → Zetanese: "I 'see frd|"
  • English: "I will eat lunch." → Zetanese: "I "et lch|"
  • English: "I ate lunch." → Zetanese: "I `et lch|"

Conclusion

NeoEng+ (Zetanese) is designed to be concise, clear, and fast. It uses symbols to mark tenses and shortens words to make communication efficient. By using this system, we can communicate quickly while maintaining clarity.
